User Researcher

Serving Your Team

We can build the right thing sooner by knowing why users behave the way they do.

Aetna

was developing a new digital price transparency healthcare tool for The Home Depot employees. To understand motivations and behavioral patterns, I assembled and led a research team to conduct store manager interviews, an online survey, and employee phone interviews. We also went to stores around the country to test a digital prototype with employees.

Outcome:
Employee insights were translated to product marketing materials. Product testing with 23 employees enabled real-time feedback to make quick product iterations.

Greater Than One

was helping a client develop a new cancer patient portal. The goal was to pinpoint a way to "create utility for cancer patients to better navigate their treatment decisions." I designed and conducted research that entailed 20 phone interviews with cancer patients and caregivers across the country; then translated needs and motivations into a patient journey map. I also facilitated concept testing with 18 patients to assess health literacy around cancer genomics.

OUTCOME:
Translating research insights into a patient journey map allowed the design team to clarify product touchpoint opportunities. Concept testing resulted in three iterations. Preliminary responses included "What is genomic testing? I don’t understand" and "Can you explain this to me?" By the end of testing, overall user response was "“The fact that you can find out about your type of cancer on a molecular level makes so much sense. It is so important" and “If this existed then we wouldn’t have to troll the entire Internet for what you needed. It's so useful.”

Whole Foods

was piloting a physical Wellness Club offering in five stores. All services were rendered in the clubs (brick and mortar) and the Club websites were purely informational. Whole Foods wanted to launch a Virtual Wellness Club where most of the member experiences would be supported by interactive digital multi-media platforms: a website, 2-4 apps, social media, etc. The vision was to develop virtual tools and support systems that "could guide and support club members in their journey to a healthier lifestyle." The goal was to learn what was needed in order to transition an in-person program to a virtual one.

OUTCOME:
110 in-person customer interviews across 10 stores and 302 online survey respondents to translate user needs and motivations into the virtual program prototype design. The prototype was then tested with 10 Whole Foods customers before launch.

Behavior Design Workshops

Inspired by scientifically validated health behavior change theories (such at the Fogg Behavior Model, Health Belief Model, Social Cognitive Theory, etc), I teach this 3-hour interactive workshop to help teams approach behavioral outcomes in a new and unified way. Participants learn the psychology behind what drives user engagement and how to build products to cater to core human needs. Behavior Design is an effective behavior change methodology blending the best practices from behavioral science and experience design. It is a systematic way of thinking about behavior change; driven by psychological research to ensure we match target behaviors with solutions for achieving those behaviors. Workshop takeaways include practical steps for developing a prototype to optimize user engagement.
